02 — Vault shares & eRate

Shares, eRate, and How Value Grows

When you deposit into a Concrete vault, you don't simply park money. You buy into a pool. In exchange, you receive vault shares — a number that represents your proportional ownership of everything inside that vault.

Analogy — The Pizza Model

Imagine a pizza that gets larger over time. When you join, you're given a slice. You don't have more slices as time goes on — but the pizza gets bigger. So your slice becomes worth more. That's how vault shares work. Your share count stays the same. The value of each share grows.

The eRate (exchange rate) is the real-time price of a single vault share, measured in the underlying asset. When the vault earns yield, the eRate goes up. When you eventually exit, your shares are redeemed at whatever the eRate is at that moment.

So when your dashboard shows a growing number — it's not new shares appearing. It's the same shares becoming more valuable, because the vault's eRate is climbing.

03 — NAV Explained

NAV: The Total Picture

NAV stands for Net Asset Value. In plain terms: it's the total value of everything inside the vault at any given moment — all the deployed capital, accrued yield, and held assets combined into one number.

Think of the vault as a shared jar of money. NAV is how full the jar is. Shares are how many people have a claim on it, and in what proportion. The eRate is simply: jar size ÷ total shares.

NAV grows when...
Yield is Generated

Strategies earn returns, accrued fees settle, and capital compounds over time.

NAV affects...
Your Share Price

As NAV increases, the eRate increases. Your same shares are now worth more underlying assets.

This is why NAV is the heartbeat of a vault. A growing NAV means the vault's strategies are working. When you see your balance increase, it's because the total pool — the NAV — has grown, making each share more valuable.

04 — The time factor

Why Time Is the Real Asset

Concrete vaults are not built for quick wins. They're built for compounding — and compounding requires time to do its work.

Here's the honest picture: yield strategies take time to execute. Capital is deployed across different positions on-chain, and those positions need time to mature and generate returns. Gas costs, execution fees, and rebalancing all factor into the equation — short-term, those friction costs can outweigh gains.

Analogy — The Garden

Planting seeds in a garden costs effort upfront. For the first few days, nothing seems to happen. Then shoots appear. Then growth accelerates. Pulling the plant out on day two doesn't let you "cut your losses" — it just means you never got the harvest. DeFi vaults work the same way.

The longer you stay in a vault, the more you benefit from compounding. Each cycle of yield gets folded back into the pool, increasing NAV, increasing eRate, making your shares worth more — which then earns yield on a larger base. That's the compounding loop working in your favor.

Withdrawals are also structured for stability. This isn't a restriction — it's a feature. When large amounts exit abruptly, it forces strategies to unwind at suboptimal moments, hurting everyone in the pool. The structure protects the integrity of the vault for all participants.

05 — Active management

The Vault Doesn't Just Sit There

Here's what separates Concrete from simply holding tokens in your wallet: the vault is actively working. Capital isn't deposited and forgotten. It's continuously deployed, monitored, and adjusted.

Analogy — The Chef

A great chef doesn't just throw ingredients in a pan and walk away. They taste, adjust seasoning, change heat, swap components based on what's available and what the dish needs. The vault operates the same way — constantly reading market conditions and repositioning capital toward better opportunities.

06 — Putting it together

How It All Connects

Now let's zoom out and see the full picture. You deposit assets. The vault pools your capital with others'. That pooled capital is deployed across strategies by active management logic — automatically compounding, rebalancing, and capturing opportunities across the market.

Your vault shares represent your proportional ownership of that growing pool. The eRate tracks how each share's value increases over time. And NAV — the total pool value — is the ultimate measure of the vault's health and performance.

The longer you participate, the more cycles of compounding work in your favor. Rebalancing captures better entries. Automated compounding eliminates drag. And the managed layer means every dollar in the vault is always working toward the best available outcome — not just sitting.
